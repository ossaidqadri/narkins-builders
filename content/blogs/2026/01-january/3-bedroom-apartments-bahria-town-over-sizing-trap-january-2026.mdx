## The Psychology of Over-Sizing: Why Families Do This

Understanding why 70% of buyers make this expensive mistake reveals powerful psychological and social forces:

**1. Joint Family Pressure**
Pakistani joint family culture creates perceived need for guest accommodations. "What will we do when your parents visit?" drives purchase decisions even when visits are rare.

**2. Status Signaling**
3-BHK apartments signal upward mobility and success. Buying 2-BHK feels like settling or downgrading, regardless of actual space needs.

**3. Future-Proofing Anxiety**
"We might have another child" or "My elderly parents might move in" lead families to buy for hypothetical futures that often don't materialize.

**4. Developer Marketing**
Sales teams position 3-BHK as the "family option" while depicting 2-BHK as suitable only for couples or small families, regardless of actual livability.

**5. Resale Value Myth**
Common belief that 3-BHK has better resale value (not supported by data when comparing price-per-square-foot appreciation rates).

The 3-BHK decision is correct for specific situations:
