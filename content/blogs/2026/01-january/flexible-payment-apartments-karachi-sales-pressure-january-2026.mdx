## The "Limited Time Offer" Illusion

The most common tactic is creating artificial scarcity. Developers know that buyers fear missing out (FOMO).
-   **The tactic:** Announcing "exclusive discounts" or "special rates" that are only valid for a few days or weeks.
-   **The reality:** These "limited-time" prices are often just the initial launch price, which will naturally increase with subsequent phases anyway. The deadline is artificial; the price will be matched by the next "special offer."

## "Only a Few Units Left" - The Inventory Game

Similarly, claims of limited inventory are often manipulative.
-   **The tactic:** Sales agents highlight only a few "prime" units remaining at the current price. They might even steer you away from your preferred unit by saying it's "just been sold."
-   **The reality:** Developers strategically release inventory to create demand. They might have hundreds of units left but are only showing you the ones they want to sell at the current rate. They can always release more units or similar inventory in the next phase at a higher price.

This makes buyers feel lucky to snag a "rare" unit, overlooking whether it's the *right* unit for their needs and budget.

## "The Price is Going Up Tomorrow!" - Payment Plan Manipulation

This is particularly common with **new apartments Bahria Town Karachi 2025** launches.
-   **The tactic:** Announcing price hikes for the next phase, pushing you to commit *now*.
-   **The reality:** The "price hike" is often baked into the payment schedule. What seems like a discount now might just be paying more over time. For example, a "zero-interest" plan might include a 20-30% markup in the total price compared to a cash payment. The agent doesn't highlight this markup, only the "low monthly installments."
