## What Digital Economy Workers Actually Need From Properties

Traditional property evaluation focused on bedrooms, bathrooms, square footage, and location proximity to offices. Digital economy transformation makes completely different features critical.

**The New Property Requirements:**

According to research on remote work shaping real estate demand, buyers now prioritize:

**Essential Infrastructure:**
- High-speed fiber-optic internet (not negotiable, not optional)
- Reliable electricity (can't work if power cuts interrupt daily)
- Backup power systems (generators or UPS for business continuity)
- Cellular coverage for mobile connectivity
- Sound insulation (video calls can't compete with street noise)

**Space Configuration:**
- Dedicated home office room (not "maybe use dining room")
- Quiet zones isolated from household activity
- Natural lighting for extended screen time
- Ergonomic workspace possibilities
- Storage for equipment and supplies

**Lifestyle Features:**
- Outdoor areas for breaks from screens
- Fitness facilities (replacing office-area gyms)
- Proximity to logistics hubs (e-commerce workers need package access)
- Parking (delivery services require vehicle access)
- Security for home-based business operations

Properties designed in 2000-2015 didn't consider any of these requirements because they didn't exist or matter. Properties designed for 2025+ must integrate all of them or lose competitive positioning.
